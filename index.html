I am a first year student here at UC Riverside, and I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Liberal Studies. I eventually want to try and work my way towards a Master's degree in Education as well. I believe that a career in education is one that is deeply rewarding and worthwhile, and that there is no greater gift than the ability to inspire young minds. I hope to instill a love for learning in my students, regardless of the subject.

Involvement in one's community is an important aspect of our lives, so I strive to apply myself to any volunteer involvement in any way I can. For example, I have working with a cat rescue organization for the past two years. This organization is known as the Feline Underground Rescue, and through them I have assisted in the adoption of numerous felines. This opportunity granted me both a heightened sense of compassion and a greater appreciation for hard work.

When I am not volunteering or in school, I can be found working on some new creation. I am self taught in animation, two-dimensional medium art, three-dimensional modeling, music composition, and music performance on various instruments. I taught myself these skills because the joy I feel from the process of raw creation is both exhilirating and therapuetic. Though these skills come with bouts of frustration, I find that these frustrations are extremely positive for my skills because they force me to work harder to overcome these problems. The results are always worth it in the end.
